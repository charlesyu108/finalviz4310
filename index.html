The Online Dating Profile

A close look into the San Francisco dating scene

Introduction

If you have ever dated anyone before 😢, you would know that dating involves people with a mutual interest in each other. But to get things started, someone needs to make the first move to get to know someone else. OkCupid is a platform that allows people to meet others. It is an American-based, internationally operating online dating friendship, and social networking website. San Francisco is an interesting location to look at since it has a unique dating culture and it is an upcoming tech scene with a large focus on work. Thanks to the data collected by Albert Y. Kim and Adriana Escobedo-Land at Reeds College on June 2012, our group was able to look at thousands of OkCupid profiles from the San Francisco area. We further processed the data to look only at the approximately 30,000 OkCupid members who were active within the last month of the date that the data was collected. Each dot represents an individual's profile and can be hovered to see the individual's self summary. The individuals represented in our data visualization are all very unique with varying age, sexual orientation, jobs, and racial profiles.

Age

Many of OkCupid users are in their mid twenties to early thirties. OkCupid members seem to be of older ages than members of other typical dating sites, like Tinder, who cater to younger audiences. Despite this, there exists younger users in their earlier 20s and older users who are beyond the age of 40. This age information is interesting given that less than 27% of 30-year-olds in San Francisco are married. It is not until age 35 that the proportion of married people in San Francisco reaches 46%.

Sexual Orientation

Here we present the sexual orientations of the users as circles with radii corresponding linearly to the proportion of members in each sexual orientation and gender bucket. The majority of OkCupid users are heterosexual; however, there exists a sizable number of individuals who identify differently, either as homosexual or biseuxal. Noticeably, there are more men than women in our OkCupid sample. It is also interesting to note that there is a sizeable gay male population but not a very large gay female population in this dataset. Yet, the number of bisexual women and gay women are roughly of the same size.

Ethnicity

The vast majority of OkCupid users from the dataset were white. Out of 25507 users, 57 percent (14529) were white which is interesting given a 2010 census stating that 48 percent of people living in San Francisco were white. Nevertheless, other sizable ethnic groups include Asians, hispanics/latinos, Black, and Indian. This is in line with the 2010 census report of people living in San Francisco with 33.3 percent of people being Asian and 6 percent of people being Black. San Francisco is special because it has such a large Asian population whereas the United States has a population of 6 percent Asians according to a 2013 census.

Jobs

Given that San Francisco was an up and coming tech location at the time of the data collection, many of the males work in Computing Industries. The culture in San Francisco is a little different from the rest of the world given the statistics about marriage stated before as there is a higher focus on work and employment. Many of the OkCupid users participated in some form of higher education like College or Graduate School and many of the users don't have children yet. The jobs are varied but there are some general trends like more men in STEM and technological sectors and more women in medicine/health and education.

Gender

Going through the previous demographics, we may have noticed that there are more men using OkCupid than females which is interesting because of a roughly 50-50 male to female population. [We hope to put in Complex Querying here].

User Responses

Now that we have gone over the variety of demographics of the OkCupid users within the dataset scraped by Albert and Adriana from Reed College, we also have information on 9 essay questions that the user has answered. The 9 essay questions are fill-in-the-blank free response questions and have no limit in length so answers may range from short sentences to paragraphs. All 9 of the essay questions ask about information relating to the user and the exact question prompts are listed as filters in the word cloud below.

We learned that the male population and female population differ in terms of demographic detail. Since OkCupid has a binary classification of sex as Male or Female, we were able to look at the top words used by Males, Females, and both Males and Females. The word cloud visualization is a result of tokenizing all of the user answers to the 9 essay questions and vectorizing the tokens in terms of word frequency in relation to the number of essays that words appeared in. The words selected were processed to ignore terms that were not interesting like common verbs, pronouns, and other words found commonly in speech.
